Our Story

Advocates for Universal Merit, AUM, is a nonprofit that was founded in order to support Monk-Life's and ThaBarWa's community members.
Here's the story of how it all started:

  • October, 2002

    Sayadaw Ashin Ottamathara Ordains

    By 2002, the founder of the ThaBarWa organization, born in Myanmar in 1969, had already successfully completed a degree in English and had been running his own chain of convenience stores, responsible for hundreds of employees. Disenchanted with his lack of involvement in helping others, he had taken up an intense, committed meditation practice three years earlier, so that in October of that year, he was ordained as a Buddhist monk and, due to the diligence of his efforts, was named a Vipassana meditation teacher by the Abbot of Devasagara Monastery in Yangon.

  • 2007

    The First Thabarwa Center Established

    Only five years after his Ordination, Sayadaw opened his first Meditation Center, a place where those following him from class to class at private homes, hospitals and monasteries could have a place to stay, practice meditation and do good deeds freely. In his center he taught a growing number of monks, nuns, yogis, patients and anyone in need to cultivate the ability of doing good deeds without limits as a means to solve problems for themselves and society.

  • 2018

    Bhante Varrapanyo Ordains

    A young American yogi, aged 20 years, began his spiritual journey in 2014 at a Zen Buddhist Meditation Center in New Mexico. His early years were marked by intensive meditation training and deep immersion into Buddhist teachings. In 2018, he made a significant move to Thailand to ordain as a Bhikkhu in the Thai Forrest Dhammayut tradition at Wat Phra Dara Philom Forrest monastery in Chiang Mai, further deepening his commitment to Buddhism. He subsequently met Sayadaw Ashin Ottamathara and serves ThaBarWa as a Dharma Teacher, focusing on alleviating others’ suffering through meditation and community.

  • 2022

    The Monk-Life Online Community is Born

    The Monk-Life Community was established online using the Discord and other platforms. It is a place for those interested in meditation, Buddhism, and lovingkindness. The server is run by a Real Buddhist Monk and Live Group Meditations are happening Daily. Anyone interested in the practice of meditation and bringing peace into their lives are welcome.

Mindfulness and Detachment

Our meditation practice emphasises focusing on the present moment, experiencing whatever happens with mindfulness and detachment.

Instruction

Freedom from Desire

Mindfulness teaches us how our behavior is governed by desire. Detachment from desire teaches us how to find true freedom.

Community

Refuge and Teamwork

With mindfulness and detachment as our guiding principles, we work together as a community to do increasing amounts of good deeds through teamwork and generosity.

Hospitals

Physical and Spiritual Care

The heart of Thabarwa has always been their "Rainbow Hospitals". The first of these was estabished in 2016 at the ThaBarWa Center in Thanlyin, Myanmar. Since then, more of these care facilties, as well as various types of clinics have been opened at multiple Thabarwa Centers across several continents.

Orphanages

A Safe Haven

The Thabarwa Centers in several countries have become known safe havens for children who have either been orphaned or abandoned. The children, sometime as early as infancy, are provided with food, shelter, clothing, housing and an education. They grow up with the community of monastics and lay practitioners serving as their extended family.
